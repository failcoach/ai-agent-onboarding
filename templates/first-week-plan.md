# First Week Plan Template

This gets generated automatically during the interview, but you can also fill it in manually. The idea: start your agent on simple, well-defined tasks and gradually increase complexity. You're calibrating, not expecting perfection.

For what's realistic at week 1, month 1, and month 3, see [guides/05-timeline.md](../guides/05-timeline.md).

## How to Pick the 3-5 Tasks

You want 3-5 specific tasks for your agent's first week, ordered simplest to hardest. "Simple" means narrow scope, clear inputs, and an output you can judge in under five minutes. Save anything that requires judgment you're not ready to delegate for week 2 or later.

**Examples, by role shape:**

- **Content / marketing agent:** draft one social post from a customer story; draft a short email based on a past one; draft a newsletter outline from last month's wins.
- **Operations / admin agent:** summarize one meeting's action items; produce a weekly team digest from your notes or Slack; flag the three highest-risk items on your pipeline.
- **Analyst / review agent:** summarize one customer support transcript; compare this month's numbers to last month's and note what changed; flag anomalies in one data export.
- **Client-facing draft agent:** draft a reply to one customer email; draft a proposal outline; draft a scoping doc for one project.

The task doesn't need to be impressive. It needs to be small enough that you can tell quickly whether the agent got it right.

# First Week Plan: [Agent Name]

## Day 1-2: Get Oriented

Goal: The agent reads everything you've given it and produces its first piece of real output. You review heavily and give detailed feedback.

- [ ] [Simple task 1 -- something the agent can do with the information it already has]
- [ ] [Simple task 2 -- another straightforward output you can review quickly]
- [ ] Review both outputs. What worked? What was off? Tell the agent directly.

## Day 3-4: Start the Routine

Goal: The agent begins doing its regular tasks. You're still reviewing everything, but it should be getting closer to what you need.

- [ ] [Core task 1 -- something from the agent's regular responsibilities]
- [ ] [Core task 2 -- another regular task, incorporating your feedback from Day 1-2]
- [ ] Review outputs. Are your corrections from Day 1-2 reflected? Give feedback on what's new.

## Day 5: Calibrate

Goal: Step back and look at the whole week. What's working? What needs to change before week 2?

- [ ] Review the week's outputs together with your agent
- [ ] Update the agent's instructions based on what you learned
- [ ] Update the agent's memory with any context that kept coming up
- [ ] Identify what to focus on in week 2

## What "Good" Looks Like After Week 1

- The agent can complete basic tasks without major errors
- You're editing its outputs, not rewriting them
- You've given feedback at least 3-4 times
- The agent's instructions have been updated at least once
- You have a clearer picture of what this agent can and can't do yet

## What's Normal After Week 1

- Outputs that are OK but not great
- Having to re-explain some context
- Feeling like it would've been faster to do it yourself (it would have been -- this week. Not next month.)
- Discovering gaps in your original brief that you didn't anticipate
- Adjusting the scope of what you asked for

All of this is part of the process. Week 2 gets better.
